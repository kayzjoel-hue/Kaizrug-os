FAQ

Questions a skeptical operator would ask.

Why is this different from buying a normal Notion template?

Because the point is not page layout. The point is runtime behavior. Work–Study OS is designed around open loops, reviews, reminders, proof, and authority separation, so the system keeps working after the novelty dies.

Is this only for expats?

No. Expats are one of the clearest use cases because relocation reveals system weakness fast, but the operating model is also for solo founders, hospitality workers, freelancers, and overloaded operators.

Do I need to be highly technical or good at automation?

No. The aim is operational clarity, not technical flexing. The system should help you see what matters, what is alive, and what needs follow-up without requiring you to become an integration engineer.

What does “living system” actually mean here?

It means the system has feedback loops. It captures open commitments, routes them into action, supports review rituals, and gives you proof of progress instead of leaving you with static pages that decay in silence.

Will this stay in Notion forever?

No. The architecture is intentionally designed so the operating model can evolve from Notion and no-code into fuller software over time, without losing the logic that makes it useful now.
